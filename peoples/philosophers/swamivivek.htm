SWAMI VIVEKANANDA

Swami Vivekananda (1863-1902) was an Indian Hindu monk and philosopher who played a significant role in the introduction of Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world. He was a disciple of the Indian mystic Ramakrishna, and is known for his teachings on the importance of self- realization, service to humanity, and the unity of all religions.
