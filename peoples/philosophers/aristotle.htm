About ARISTOTLE

Aristotle (384 BC - 322 BC) was a Greek philosopher and scientist who was one of the most influential figures in Western philosophy. He was a student of Plato and a teacher of Alexander the Great. Aristotle's ideas and thoughts have had a profound impact on fields such as logic, metaphysics, ethics, politics, and biology, and his works continue to be studied and debated to this day.
