About MOTHER TERESA

Mother Teresa, also known as Saint Teresa of Calcutta, was a Catholic nun and missionary who dedicated her life to serving the poor and sick. She was bom in Albania in 1910 and moved to India in 1929, where she founded the Missionaries of Charity, an organization dedicated to helping the poor and sick. She was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979 and was canonized as a saint by the Catholic Church in 2016, Mother Teresa passed away in 1997 at the age of 87.
