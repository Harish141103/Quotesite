About GEORGE WASHINGTON CARVER

George Washington Carver was an American scientist, botanist, and inventor who is best known for his work on promoting alternative crops to cotton, such as peanuts and sweet potatoes, which had a profound impact on agriculture in the southern United States. He was born into slavery in Diamond, Missouri, around 1864 and died on January 5, 1943, in Tuskegee, Alabama.
