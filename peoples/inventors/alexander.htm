About ALEXANDER GRAHAM BELL

Alexander Graham Bell was a Scottish-born American inventor, scientist, and engineer who is credited with inventing the telephone, as well as contributing to the development of aeronautics and hydrofoils. He was born on March 3, 1847, in Edinburgh, Scotland, and died on August 2, 1922, in Baddeck, Nova Scotia, Canada
