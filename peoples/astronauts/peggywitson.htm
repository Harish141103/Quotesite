About PEGGY WHITSON

Peggy Whitson is an American astronaut and biochemist who has spent a total of 665 days in space over three missions, making her one of the most experienced astronauts in history. During her time in space, she has conducted a wide range of experiments and research projects, including investigations into the effects of microgravity on the human body. She has also held several leadership positions within NASA, including serving as the chief of the astronaut office.
