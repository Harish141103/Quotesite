About MAE JEMISON

Mae Jemison is an American physician, astronaut, and engineer who became the first Black woman to travel to space when she served as a mission specialist aboard the Space Shuttle Endeavour in 1992. Prior to her career as an astronaut, Jemison earned degrees in both chemical engineering and medicine, and worked as a general practitioner and a Peace Corps medical officer. She is also an advocate for science education and literacy, and has founded several organizations to promote those causes.
