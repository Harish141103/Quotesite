About SALLY RIDE

Sally Ride was an American astronaut and physicist who became the first American woman in space in 1983 as part of the Space Shuttle Challenger mission. She flew on a second space mission in 1984 and went on to work at NASA in various roles, including as a member of the Rogers Commission investigating the Space Shuttle Challenger disaster. She was also a science writer and education advocate, and founded Sally Ride Science to encourage young people, especially girls, to pursue careers in science and technology.
