About KALPANA CHAWLA

Kalpana Chawla was an Indian-American astronaut who was the first woman of Indian origin to go to space. She was selected by NASA in 1994 and completed her first space mission in 1997 as a mission specialist on the Space Shuttle Columbia. She tragically died in 2003 along with the rest of the crew during re-entry after completing her second space mission.
