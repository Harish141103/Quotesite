About BERNADETTE DEVLIN

Bernadette Devlin (McAliskey) is a Northern Irish activist and politician who emerged as a prominent figure during the civil rights movement in the late 1960s. At the age of 21, she became the youngest woman ever elected to the British Parliament. Throughout her life, Devlin has remained a vocal advocate for social justice, civil rights, and peace in Northern Ireland and around the world.
