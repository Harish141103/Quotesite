About B.R.AMBEDKAR

B.R. Ambedkar (1891-1956) was a prominent Indian jurist, economist, and social reformer who played a key role in drafting the Indian Constitution. He is also known as the father of the Indian Constitution and the leader of the Dalit community (formerly known as the “untouchables”). Ambedkar spent his life fighting against caste discrimination and social inequality in India.
