About BENJAMIN FRANKLIN

Benjamin Franklin (1706-1790) was an American statesman, scientist, philosopher, and writer. He was one of the Founding Fathers of the United States and played a key role in the American Revolution. Franklin was also a renowned inventor, known for his experiments with electricity and for inventing the lightning rod, bifocal glasses, and the Franklin stove.
