About STEPHEN HAWKING

Stephen Hawking was a British theoretical physicist and cosmologist who made significant contributions to our understanding of black holes, the Big Bang, and the nature of the universe. Despite being diagnosed with a rare motor neuron disease at the age of 21 and being given only a few years to live, he continued to work on his research and became a leading figure in the scientific community.
